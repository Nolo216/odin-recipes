Spaghetti with Meatballs

Home

Description

Spaghetti is a long, thin, solid, cylindrical pasta. It is a staple food of traditional Italian cuisine. Like other pastas, spaghetti is made of milled wheat (sometimes enriched with vitamins and minerals) and water. Italian spaghetti is typically made from durum-wheat semolina. The pasta is usually white because refined flour is used, but whole wheat flour may be added.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb spaghetti noodles
  • 1 lb ground beef (for meatballs)
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp Italian seasoning
  • 24 oz jar marinara sauce
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Steps

  1. Prepare the Meatballs: In a large bowl, combine ground beef, breadcrumbs, milk, egg, Parmesan, garlic, and Italian seasoning. Roll into 1-inch balls.
  2. Cook the Meatballs: In a large skillet, brown the meatballs on all sides over medium heat until cooked through (about 10 minutes).
  3. Simmer: Pour the marinara sauce over the meatballs, reduce heat, and simmer for 15 minutes.
  4. Boil the Pasta: Meanwhile, boil a large pot of salted water and cook the spaghetti according to package directions until al dente.
  5. Assemble: Drain the pasta and plate it. Top with a generous amount of meatballs and sauce.
  6. Serve: Garnish with fresh parsley and extra Parmesan cheese. Enjoy!
