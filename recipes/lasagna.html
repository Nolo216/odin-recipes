Lasagna

Description

Also known by the plural form lasagne, is a type of pasta made in wide, flat sheets. It originates in Italian cuisine, where it is served in a number of ways, including in broth (lasagne in brodo), but is best known for its use in a baked dish made by stacking layers of pasta, alternating with fillings such as ragù (ground meats and tomato sauce), béchamel sauce, vegetables, cheeses (which may include ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan), and seasonings and spices. Typically, cooked pasta is assembled with the other ingredients, topped with grated cheese, and then baked in an oven (al forno): regional variations of this dish are found across Italy.

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 lb Italian sausage
  • 24 oz marinara sauce
  • 15 oz ricotta cheese
  • 1 large egg
  • 4 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 tsp salt and 1/2 tsp black pepper

Steps

  1. Preparation: Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Boil lasagna noodles until al dente, drain, and set aside.
  2. Meat Sauce: Brown the ground beef and Italian sausage in a large skillet. Drain fat, then stir in the marinara sauce and simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Cheese Mixture: In a bowl, combine ricotta, egg, 1/2 cup Parmesan, parsley, salt, and pepper.
  4. Assembly: Spread 1 cup of meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Layer with noodles, 1/3 of the ricotta mixture, and 1 cup of mozzarella.
  5. Repeat: Repeat the layers twice more (sauce, noodles, ricotta, mozzarella).
  6. Bake: Top with remaining mozzarella and Parmesan. Cover with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Remove foil and bake for 15 more minutes until golden.
  7. Rest: Let the lasagna rest for 15 minutes before slicing and serving.
